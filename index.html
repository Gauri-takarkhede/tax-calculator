Frequently asked questions

Gross annual income refers to the total amount of money earned by an individual in a year before any deductions, such as taxes or expenses, are taken into account.

Extra income sources include any additional earnings beyond regular salary, such as bonuses, commissions, investment dividends, rental income, or income from freelance work.

Deductions are amounts that can be subtracted from your total income to reduce your taxable income. These deductions can include expenses like mortgage interest, charitable contributions, medical expenses, and education expenses.

Tax calculations depend on various factors such as income level, age group, and applicable tax rates. Generally, higher incomes and different age groups may be subject to different tax rates.

Tax-saving options may include investments in tax-saving instruments such as retirement plans, health savings accounts, education savings accounts, and eligible deductions as per tax laws.

Incorrect tax filing may lead to penalties or fines imposed by tax authorities. It's essential to file taxes accurately and on time to avoid penalties.
